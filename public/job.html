Empowering Individuals with Disabilities

Creating a more inclusive online experience

There are a variety of job roles dedicated to supporting disabled individuals. These roles span across healthcare, education, technology, advocacy, and more.

What accessibility features are available on the website?

Our website is designed with various accessibility features such as screen reader compatibility, keyboard navigation, and adjustable font sizes.

How can I request additional assistance while using the website?

If you require additional assistance while using our website, please contact our support team.

Are there any resources available for learning how to navigate the website?

Yes, we provide tutorials and guides on how to navigate our website effectively. You can find these resources in the 'Help' section of our website.

Can I customize the website to suit my specific needs?

Yes, you can customize certain aspects of the website such as color contrast, text size, and layout to better suit your individual needs. Look for the 'Accessibility Settings' option on the website.

Is the website compatible with assistive technologies?

Our website is designed to be compatible with a wide range of assistive technologies such as screen readers, voice recognition software, and alternative input devices.

"SPEECH LANGUAGE PATHOLOGIST"

A speech-language pathologist helps children and adults achieve good communication tactics. They do this by meeting with clients to work on exercises for lisps and other conditions like swallowing disorders.

"PHYSICAL THERAPIST"

A physical therapist works as part of a hospital or clinic to help patients practice beneficial exercises. Physical therapists typically work with patients who suffer from chronic pain or illness, but they may also work with people with disabilities.
